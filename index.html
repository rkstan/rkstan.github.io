I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Boston University's Department of Earth and Environment. I work on the GLanCE project, which aims to map and estimate 21st century global land cover, land use and land cover change at 30m resolution. Previously, I was a NASA Earth and Space Science fellow and a graduate student at Boston University where I worked with Dr. Mark Friedl. I was a member of the Land Cover and Surface Climate Group.

I study climate variability and human transformation of the landscape, especially through agriculture. I use high performance computing, machine learning and time series analysis of remote sensing observations to characterize land use and land cover change.


For updates and more information follow me on ResearchGate and Google Scholar.
